Interview ProjectThis project evaluates your technical skill, design sensibility, AI fluency, and ability to think like a product builder, not just a coder.The Brief / IntroductionThe world’s largest shopping malls are no longer just places to shop. They are massive, mixed-use destinations that combine retail, dining, entertainment, hospitality, and live events under one roof, often spanning millions of square feet and attracting tens of millions of visitors per year. Such properties operate more like small cities than traditional shopping centers. They host concert venues, theme parks, aquariums, luxury wings, convention spaces, and year-round programming that draws foot traffic no standalone retailer could generate on its own.For a property like this, the sales process is high-stakes. The commercial team is constantly pitching to prospective retail tenants (from luxury flagships to pop-up shops), corporate sponsors and brand partners (who want to activate inside the property), and event promoters and producers (who want to book the property’s venues for concerts, product launches, conventions, and more). Today, that pitch process is fragmented: a sales rep might pull up a YouTube video, flip through a static PDF, open a separate spreadsheet of demographics, and verbally narrate the story of the property. It’s manual, inconsistent, and doesn’t convey the scale or energy of what these properties actually are.Your assignment is to build the tool that replaces all of that.You will select one of the world’s largest shopping malls as your subject and build a fully interactive, browser-based sales deck for that property. Think of it as a cross between a high-end pitch deck, a luxury brand website, and an immersive destination experience, all in one. It should be a self-contained web application that a salesperson can screen-share on a live call or send as a standalone link that a prospect can explore on their own, with no one there to walk them through it.The tool’s primary audience is prospective tenants, sponsors, and event partners; decision- makers at brands, agencies, and production companies who are evaluating whether to invest in a presence at this property. The tool needs to make them feel the scale, the energy, and the commercial opportunity immediately. Within seconds, the viewer should understand why this property is different and why they need to be part of it. Every section should move them closer to a specific business action: signing a lease, committing to a sponsorship, or booking a venue.This is not a static website or a slide deck exported to HTML. It is a purpose-built interactive sales tool designed to tell the property’s story through video, data, imagery, and narrative; with the visual polish of a luxury brand and the interactivity of a modern web experience. The user controls their own journey through the content (non-linear navigation), video is the primary storytelling medium (not decoration), and the overall experience should feel cinematic, confident, and impossible to ignore.Choose Your SubjectSelect any of the world’s largest shopping malls as the subject of your project.
